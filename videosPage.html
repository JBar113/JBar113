3/2/2020 Update:

Work in progress for a character I have collaborated on with another designer. This is a draft of the protagonist for a short project we are developing. We wanted to be able to see the character in 3d in order to refine his design further, and to have a model that we could begin making prototype poses and animation with.

The character was designed by us both. Pictured below are some early sketches I did of various parts of his outfit. I then handed off my work to my partner so that he could produce a full draft of his design.

After getting back the design art, I created a low-poly model and then refined it into a high-poly sculpt.

Low poly draft model beside the concept sketch provided by my partner.
Overlapping image and model. Slight modifications were made to proportions and accessories.
Closeup of low-poly draft.
High-poly sculpt of face and clothes.

Finally, simple textures were put in place using the palette we decided upon. Most of the colors are simple and solid because we are just trying to get a feel for the character, although the face has more defined detail.

Early bird animations. In addition to hopping and flying, it has a "hit" animation where it gets partially flattened. This would be used if an object were thrown at it.

Early wizard animations. This enemy will not need as many animations as it couldn't be knocked over. Instead it will hover above the ground, cast magic, and only die if hit by a thrown object.
